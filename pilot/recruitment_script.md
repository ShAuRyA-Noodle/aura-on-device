# Aura Pilot — Recruitment Scripts

Three recruitment surfaces for the n=30 quantitative + n=8 qualitative pilot. Sourced from Thapar campus (Patiala) plus a small Bangalore segment for city balance. **Unpaid.** All scripts say so up-front.

Last updated: 2026-05-07
Owner: Shaurya Punj

---

## Script A — WhatsApp message template (1:1 or small group)

Three short paragraphs. Plain. No hype. No emojis. No "revolutionary" anything.

---

> Hey [Name],
>
> Shorya and I are building a thing called Aura for the Samsung EnnovateX hackathon — a small on-phone assistant that tries to quiet your group chats, summarise your morning, and notice when you are stressed. We need real users to test it for 7 days, and you came to mind.
>
> Honest version: it runs on iPhone (TestFlight), it is unpaid, and the time cost is your normal phone use for 7 days plus either a 60-minute interview or a 30-minute task session at the end. All data stays on your phone. We never see your messages or your bank stuff. You can quit any day without explaining.
>
> If you are open to it, reply yes and I will send the consent form and a slot link. If you are not, no worries at all — just tell me and we move on. Thanks for reading either way.
>
> — Shaurya

---

**Send-cadence rule:** one send per person per week. If they do not reply, do not double-tap. Do not add to a broadcast list. Do not screenshot replies.

## Script B — Hostel-corridor poster (A4)

One A4 sheet. Printed on the cheapest matte paper from Thapar's print shop. Pinned on hostel notice boards (Boys' Hostel B/C/D, Girls' Hostel A/B), the ECE block corridor, and the LP4 / LP3 study-room doors.

### Layout description

- **Format**: A4 portrait, 297 × 210 mm.
- **Background**: warm off-white (#FAF8F5).
- **Margins**: 25 mm all sides.

- **Hero number**: the digit `30` in Fraunces 240 pt, ink black, top-centre, baseline at ~70 mm from top. This is the only "loud" element — sized to be readable from across the corridor.

- **Tagline directly below the 30**: in Inter Tight 28 pt, tracked +20.
  > spots open. iPhone. unpaid. seven days.

- **Mid-block paragraph** (Inter Tight 14 pt, line height 22 pt, ~120 mm from top, max 90 characters wide):
  > Two ECE students are testing an on-phone assistant called Aura. We want to see if it actually quiets your day or just adds another notification. 7 days of normal phone use, then either a 60-minute chat or a 30-minute task session. All data stays on your phone. No payment. No catch. Hackathon entry, not a startup.

- **QR code**: 50 × 50 mm, ink black on warm off-white, bottom-centre at ~210 mm from top, links to the Google Form sign-up sheet. Caption underneath in Inter Tight 12 pt: `bit.ly/aura-pilot-thapar`.

- **Footer line** (Inter Tight 10 pt, bottom margin):
  > Galaxy Brain — Shaurya Punj (ECE, 3rd year), Shorya Gupta (CompE, 2nd year) — workwithshaurya10@gmail.com

- **Single accent**: a thin sunset-orange (#FF5B2E) horizontal hairline, 1 pt, between the tagline and the mid-block. Nothing else in colour.

### Banned elements
- No phone illustration.
- No brain graphic.
- No stock photo.
- No exclamation mark.
- No "AI-powered" or any banned word from `plan.md` §5.3.

### Print run
- 12 copies. 4 hostels × 2 boards + 4 academic blocks. Replace weekly.

## Script C — Discord / Slack post (Thapar tech community)

For the **Thapar Tech Society Discord**, **Thapar CSI Slack**, and the **ThaparHacks WhatsApp community**. Same body for each, posted in the `#general` or `#opportunities` channel.

```
title: looking for 30 + 8 friends to break our hackathon project (unpaid)

Shaurya (ECE 3rd) and Shorya (CompE 2nd) — Galaxy Brain team for
Samsung EnnovateX 2026.

We built Aura — an on-phone assistant that tries to quiet your
notifications, summarise your morning, and flag UPI weirdness. It
runs on iPhone via TestFlight. All compute is on-device. Nothing
goes to the cloud.

We need:
  - 30 people for a 30-minute task session  (after 7 days of use)
  - 8 people for a 60-minute interview      (after 7 days of use)

The 7 days are just normal phone use plus a 90-second daily diary.

Ground rules, said up-front:
  - unpaid. academic / hackathon, not a company.
  - iPhone only. TestFlight will eat ~3 GB.
  - your data stays on your phone. consent form has full detail.
  - quit any day. no questions.

Ideal mix: ECE / CompE / mixed branches, 50/50 gender, hostel + day
scholar, plus 2 Bangalore-based folks for city balance.

If interested, react with anything and DM me — I will send the
consent form and a slot link.

— shaurya
```

### Posting rule
- Post once per channel. Do not bump. Do not cross-post the same day.
- If a thread starts, answer technical questions inline. Do not pitch.
