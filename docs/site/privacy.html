The five privacy promises

Five plain-English promises. Each is tested against the threat model and tied to a specific architecture commitment in the ADR index.

  1. Your data lives on your device.

    Aura processes notification metadata, Gmail metadata, calendar events, SMS (Android only), HRV, sleep, and typing-entropy buckets on the phone. The encrypted memory graph stays on your device. There is no api.aura.ai; we do not operate a backend. ADR-0005.

  2. Nothing leaves the device unless you press export.

    No background sync of personal data. The only network calls Aura makes are OAuth token exchange, model weight download on first install, and Distance Matrix queries when computing a Leave-By Alert. Memory graph export to JSON is share-sheet driven; you pick the destination.

  3. Every action shows its work, and you can erase the work.

    Every autonomous or semi-autonomous Aura action emits a Reasoning Trace — trigger, signals, candidates, chosen, rationale, outcome. Inspect any trace, delete a trace, a node, a time-range, or the whole graph in one tap. Daily Merkle roots in Settings let you verify the audit log has not been retroactively edited. ADR-0004.

  4. Aura speaks at most three times a day, and you can take that to zero.

    A named state variable, the Silence Budget, caps proactive non-safety surfaces at 3 per local day. Each surface decrements the budget by one token. Tapping Useful on a card refunds one token, capped at the daily ceiling. The default is configurable from 1 to 5. ADR-0003.

  5. Five rapid taps wipe everything, including from us.

    The panic-wipe gesture (five rapid presses of the side button within three seconds) destroys the SQLCipher key, revokes OAuth refresh tokens, deletes the app sandbox, and relaunches Aura into fresh-install state. There is a three-second hold-cancel window. Nothing is logged about the wipe — that absence is a feature.

What we explicitly do not promise

  • We do not promise unbreakable privacy. The OS itself is the floor.
  • We do not promise that a determined coercer cannot extract every secret from the user.
  • We do not promise zero false positives in the orchestrator's decisions. Every action is reversible.
  • We do not promise no notifications. We promise at most three proactive non-safety surfaces a day under default settings.
